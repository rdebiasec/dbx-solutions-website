**Title:** Customer demand grows faster than manual follow-up can keep up.

1. **Slow responses** — Customers move on when questions sit unanswered during busy hours or after closing.
2. **Repetitive questions** — Your team spends time repeating answers that could be handled consistently.
3. **Missed leads** — High-intent inquiries lose momentum when follow-up is delayed or incomplete.
4. **Disconnected tools** — Conversation details often stay separate from the CRM, calendar, support queue, or your team's daily work.
5. **Limited team capacity** — Growing demand creates more customer conversations than a lean team can manage manually.
6. **Customer frustration** — Slow or inconsistent replies erode trust—customers feel ignored even when your team is doing their best behind the scenes.

A: Yes. WhatsApp can be part of the mix along with web chat, SMS, forms, and email, depending on your setup.

A: Yes. DBX designs escalation paths so your team can review, respond, or take over when judgment matters.

**Q: How long does implementation take?**  
A: Timing depends on scope and integrations. Many businesses start with one focused use case, then expand after launch.

**Q: How are AI responses controlled?**  
A: Replies are built from approved business information, clear rules, escalation paths, and ongoing review of real chats.

**Q: Can DBX connect with our existing tools?**  
A: In many cases, yes. DBX can connect conversations with CRMs, calendars, support tools, forms, and team alerts.

A: No. The goal is to support your team, reduce repetitive work, and keep people focused on higher-value customer needs.
